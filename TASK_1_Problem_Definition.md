# Task 1: Problem Definition & Audience

## Problem Statement (1 sentence)

High-income earners ($100K-$500K+) without financial backgrounds struggle to bridge the gap between accumulating wealth and managing it strategically, resulting in misaligned investments, missed tax-optimization opportunities, and delayed progress toward wealth-building goals.

---

## Why This Is a Problem (1-2 paragraphs)

### Who Has the Problem?
**Primary Persona:** Non-finance professional earners (software engineers, doctors, attorneys, executives) aged 28-55 who have:
- Accumulated meaningful savings ($100K-$5M+)
- Multiple income streams or equity compensation (stock options, RSUs, partnerships)
- Complex tax situations
- Undefined or reactive financial strategy

These individuals are **intelligent and analytical** in their domain but **financially unsophisticated** because their profession doesn't require it. They are **decision-makers** but lack the mental models to make confident financial decisions.

### What Are They Trying to Do?
They're asking themselves:
- "How much should I actually keep in cash? Invest? Where?"
- "Is my portfolio even aligned with my timeline and risk tolerance?"
- "Am I paying more taxes than I need to?"
- "How do I think about debt when I have investment options?"
- "What does retirement security actually look like for someone like me?"
- "Am I making emotional vs. rational financial decisions?"

**The real gap:** They can *calculate* returns, but they can't *think about* financial strategy. They lack a **decision framework**.

### How Do They Handle It Today?
1. **Paralysis by analysis** — Spend 40+ hours researching, reading blogs, watching YouTube (Bogleheads, BigLaw Investor, etc.) but still unsure if their approach is right
2. **Fire-and-forget investing** — Pick a target-date fund and hope, ignoring optimization opportunities
3. **Fragmented advice** — Piece together knowledge from Reddit, podcasts, books (Atomic Habits, The Millionaire Next Door) but no coherent strategy
4. **Hire traditional advisors** — Cost 0.5-1.5% AUM ($500-$5K+/year minimum), require high account minimums, may have conflicts of interest
5. **Rely on employer 401k defaults** — Convenient but often suboptimal (high expense ratios, poor diversification)
6. **Avoid the decision** — Leave significant money in checking/savings earning 0.01%, paralyzed by choice

### Why Isn't That Good Enough?
- **Advisor accessibility gap** — Financial advisors are gatekept for $500K+ portfolios; RIAs/fee-only advisors still cost $1.5K-5K/year
- **No personalized framework** — Generic "90% stocks / 10% bonds" doesn't account for their specific situation (unvested equity, tax bracket, career risk)
- **Information fragmentation** — No integrated source that connects their situation → strategy → action
- **Behavioral blind spots** — They don't know what they don't know (tax-loss harvesting, asset location strategy, behavioral finance principles)
- **Analysis paralysis tax** — Hours spent researching = opportunity cost, and they still lack conviction
- **Reactive vs. strategic** — They're managing money, not building wealth intentionally

## Current Workflow Diagram (How High-Income Earners Approach Financial Decisions Today)

```
Financial Decision / Goal Emerges
(e.g., "Should I max out my 401k or invest more in taxable account?")
         |
         v
   [ Analysis Phase: 4-12 hours ]
   |
   +-- Search: "401k vs taxable account" (Google, Reddit)
   +-- Read: Bogleheads wiki, BigLaw Investor blog
   +-- Watch: YouTube (Graham Stephan, Two Cents)
   +-- Spreadsheet: Build personal model / calculator
   +-- Ask: Peer with similar situation
   |
   v
   [ Synthesis Phase: Conflicting Mental Models ]
   |
   +--> "Max 401k" (risk-averse, tax-deferral focus)
   +--> "Tax-loss harvest instead" (optimization focus)
   +--> "Don't max if you can't access funds" (liquidity focus)
   +--> "Do both: max 401k AND invest in taxable" (max wealth focus)
   |
   v
   [ Decision Phase: Conviction Gap ]
   |
   +--> Anxiety: "Am I leaving money on the table?"
   +--> Regret: "If the market crashes, was this right?"
   +--> Avoidance: "Maybe I'll decide later..."
   |
   v
   [ Outcome: Suboptimal or Delayed Decision ]
```

### Critical Pain Points in Current Workflow:

| Pain Point | Impact | Why It Happens |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| 🔴 **No Decision Framework** | Decisions feel arbitrary; hard to explain to spouse/partner | Different sources use different mental models |
| 🔴 **Scattered Information** | 8+ hours researching, synthesizing across 10+ sources | No single source tailored to their situation |
| 🔴 **Missing Personal Context** | Generic 60/40 allocation ignores RSU vesting, tax bracket, job risk | Advice doesn't account for their specific cash flows or equity compensation |
| 🔴 **Blind Spots** | Leaves money on table (tax-loss harvesting, asset location, Medicare IRMAA strategy) | They don't know what questions to ask |
| 🔴 **No Accountability** | Makes decision, then second-guesses it during market volatility | Lacks a coherent narrative for "why" the strategy is right |
| 🔴 **Behavioral Trap** | Procrastinates on optimization (holding $200k in 0% savings account) | Complexity → paralysis → inaction is "safe" |
| 🔴 **Advisor Cost Barrier** | Can't afford fee-only advisor ($2-5k/year) without $500k AUM | Traditional advisors are gatekept for wealthy; fee-only advisors still expensive |
| 🔴 **Conflict of Interest Risk** | Advisor recommends expensive funds or products with high fees | Commission-based advisors have incentive misalignment |

### What a Financial Advisor Would Do Instead:
1. **Establish constraints** — Timeline, risk tolerance, major upcoming expenses
2. **Define the goal** — Retirement date, target wealth, financial independence number
3. **Map the situation** — Income, debt, assets, tax bracket, job/equity risk
4. **Create a decision framework** — Asset allocation, funding priority, tax strategy
5. **Communicate the "why"** — So the decision sticks through market volatility
6. **Monitor & adjust** — Rebalance, tax-loss harvest, adapt to life changes

## Evaluation Questions & Input-Output Pairs

### Tier 1: Foundation Questions (Establishing Mental Model)

1. **"I just got a $150k bonus. What should I do with it?"**
   - Expected output: Decision tree accounting for: debt, emergency fund size, tax bracket impact, current asset allocation gaps, timeline to major goals
   - Why this matters: Bonus inflection points are critical; non-advisors often either spend it or leave it in cash

2. **"What's a realistic asset allocation for someone 35 years old with $500k, retiring at 60, and a $200k stable salary?"**
   - Expected output: Framework for: time horizon → risk capacity → risk tolerance alignment, adjusted for income stability and major expenses
   - Why this matters: Age-based allocation is outdated; what matters is time horizon + other income sources

3. **"I have $100k in employer stock. Should I diversify or hold?"**
   - Expected output: Framework for: concentration risk vs tax consequences, employer-specific context, behavioral anchoring bias, staged diversification approach
   - Why this matters: High-income earners often over-concentrate in employer stock; needs behavioral + tax analysis

### Tier 2: Strategy & Optimization Questions

4. **"I'm earning $250k, maxing my 401k, and have $100k left to invest. Should I contribute to a backdoor Roth or invest in taxable?"**
   - Expected output: Decision framework accounting for: tax brackets, Phase-in thresholds (Medicare IRMAA, ACA subsidy cliffs), future income trajectory, estate planning
   - Why this matters: This is where optimization happens; easy to miss $10-20k/year in unnecessary taxes

5. **"I'm $200k in student debt and have $300k in savings. Should I pay off the loans or invest it?"**
   - Expected output: Integrated decision: interest rate arbitrage, behavioral anchoring, psychological payoff timeline, integration with overall financial goal
   - Why this matters: Textbook says "if rate < 5%, invest"; reality requires personal financial psychology + time horizon alignment

6. **"How do I think about taxable account asset location vs. my 401k and Roth IRA?"**
   - Expected output: Framework for: tax-efficient placement (bonds in tax-deferred, stocks in taxable), dividend vs growth stocks, tax-loss harvesting opportunities
   - Why this matters: Asset location is a hidden optimization that saves $3-5k/year for six-figure portfolios

### Tier 3: Behavioral & Psychological Questions

7. **"I have $50k in a high-yield savings account earning 5%. The stock market keeps hitting all-time highs. Should I invest it?"**
   - Expected output: Address: FOMO + sequence-of-returns risk, emergency fund adequacy, risk tolerance honest assessment, dollar-cost averaging option
   - Why this matters: Emotions drive bad timing; need framework to override behavioral biases

8. **"My portfolio is down 15% and I'm stressed. Should I rebalance or wait for a recovery?"**
   - Expected output: Rebalancing framework, historical perspective on drawdowns, behavioral coaching, plan reassurance
   - Why this matters: Market downturns test conviction; need mechanism to prevent panic selling

## Notes for Solution Design

### What a Financial Advisor Actually Does:
1. **Asks clarifying questions** — Gather situation data (income, assets, timeline, risk tolerance, goals)
2. **Builds a mental model** — Creates shared understanding of constraints + objectives
3. **Proposes a framework** — Actionable decision rules, not generic advice
4. **Explains the "why"** — So client understands reasoning and can execute with conviction
5. **Handles objections** — Addresses fears, misconceptions, behavioral biases
6. **Monitors** — Checks in periodically to adjust as circumstances change

### Key Requirements for This Solution:
- ✅ **Contextual** — Ask clarifying questions; don't give generic advice (different answer for 35-year-old $100k earner vs 45-year-old $500k earner)
- ✅ **Integrated** — Connect decisions (e.g., "maxing 401k" affects Roth backdoor strategy, tax bracket, AMT, Medicare IRMAA)
- ✅ **Behavioral** — Acknowledge psychological factors (FOMO, loss aversion, analysis paralysis)
- ✅ **Framework-based** — Teach decision rules, not just answers ("here's how to think about X")
- ✅ **Disclaimer-aware** — Be clear about what's guidance vs. what requires CPA/attorney
- ✅ **Source-grounded** — Ground recommendations in "Were-Talking-Millions.pdf" + research
- ✅ **Browser-runnable** — Work on phone/laptop, low friction to access
- ✅ **Memory-aware** — Remember user's situation across conversation (don't re-ask same questions)

### Out of Scope (Will politely redirect):
- ❌ Specific stock/fund recommendations (e.g., "Buy VTSAX")
- ❌ Real portfolio management / execution
- ❌ Tax preparation or CPA-level tax planning
- ❌ Legal advice (estate planning, incorporation, etc.)
- ❌ Insurance product sales
- ❌ Behavioral therapy for financial anxiety (encourage professional help)

### Solution Success Metric:
A user should be able to ask a complex question (e.g., "I have $250k bonus, $500k saved, $200k student debt, retiring at 60, should I max Roth backdoor or invest in taxable?") and receive a **decision framework** — not a yes/no answer, but a clear "here's how to think about it" that they can explain to their spouse, revisit annually, and adapt as their situation changes.
